Save parts

Launch SolidWorks and open a sheet metal part (.sldprt).

Then follow these steps:

  1. Switch to the TruSmartCAM tab in SolidWorks to access the TruSmartCAM toolbar.

  2. Click Save to TruSmartCAM on the TruSmartCAM toolbar.

In the Save to TruSmartCAM dialog, select the appropriate TruSmartCAM raw material, then click OK to upload and save the part to the TruSmartCAM part library.

Part will be now saved in TruSmartCAM.

TruSmartCAM includes a few sample parts located at C:\Program Files\Metamation\ TruSmartCAM \Samples\Parts. You can use it for testing purpose if needed.

Auto material resolution

This feature automatically converts SolidWorks materials and part thickness values into the corresponding TruSmartCAM raw materials during upload.

To configure material mapping:

  1. Click Options on the TruSmartCAM toolbar to open the Options dialog.

  2. Navigate to the Material Mapping page.

  3. In the left pane, select a SolidWorks material.

  4. In the right pane, select the corresponding TruSmartCAM material.

  5. Click Map to create the mapping.

    • The mapped pair appears in the mapping list at the bottom.

  6. To remove a mapping, select it and click Un-map.

Thickness fudge setting

The thickness fudge value defines the tolerance range within which TruSmartCAM matches the sheet metal model thickness from SolidWorks to an existing raw material thickness in the TruSmartCAM database. It compensates for small variations in model thickness caused by design rounding, unit conversions, etc…​

When the SolidWorks part thickness does not exactly match a material in TruSmartCAM, the fudge value ensures that a nearest available material is selected automatically.

Example

With a nominal 2.0 mm material and a fudge value of 0.05 mm:

  • TruSmartCAM accepts any SolidWorks model thickness between 1.95 mm and 2.05 mm as a valid match.

  • Deviations beyond this range will trigger a manual material selection prompt during part upload.

To view or modify the tolerance value:

  • Click on Options on the TruSmartCAM toolbar and then navigate to Misc. settings to view the tolerance value, now click on Save to TruSmartCAM.

  • In the Save to TruSmartCAM dialog click OK, the part will be saved to TruSmartCAM.

Reading custom part properties

This setting allows TruSmartCAM to automatically read custom part information—such as Material, Revision, and other user-defined attributes—directly from SolidWorks part files.

To set it up:

  • Click Options on the TruSmartCAM toolbar to open the Options dialog and then navigate to Custom Fields.

  • Use the Map and Un-map buttons to link the required TruSmartCAM fields with the corresponding SolidWorks Custom or Configuration fields.

Values displayed are loaded from the currently active SolidWorks part.

  • Once mapping is complete, the selected custom properties will be automatically read from SolidWorks whenever parts are imported or updated in TruSmartCAM, now click Save to TruSmartCAM.

The mappings defined here are saved in the TruSmartCAM repository and automatically synchronized across all TruSmartCAM SolidWorks installations within the network.

  • In the Save to TruSmartCAM dialog click on OK, the part will be saved to TruSmartCAM.
